# Perma-Worker Cooperative Business Documentation

A practical, conversion-ready business administration repository for launching and operating a Michigan permaculture services business that begins as an LLC and is structured to convert into a worker cooperative when the business reaches operational readiness.

> **Important:** This repository is a high-quality operational draft, not legal or tax advice. Before filing, signing, publishing, or using any document with workers, customers, contractors, or regulators, review the full package with a Michigan business attorney and a CPA or enrolled agent.

## Purpose

This repository is designed to help a founder-led permaculture business operate with disciplined records, coherent governance, clear ownership of intellectual property, consistent customer-facing policies, and a deliberate pathway into worker ownership.

The suite is structured to support a business that may:
- provide permaculture consulting, design, implementation support, training, workshops, and maintenance,
- sell digital products, templates, educational materials, and software-enabled services,
- hire employees and engage contractors,
- protect brand assets and assign IP into the company,
- maintain board- and audit-ready records, and
- convert from a Michigan LLC into a Michigan cooperative or employee-owned structure when scale, revenue, and staffing justify it.

## Recommended adoption order

1. Finalize the context folder.
2. File LLC formation documents and obtain EIN.
3. Adopt the operating agreement and initial capitalization schedule.
4. Assign founder-created IP into the LLC.
5. Publish or adopt business policies used in operations.
6. Maintain the decision log and minutes policy from day one.
7. Use the governance evolution policy to review readiness quarterly.
8. When conversion becomes realistic, finalize the cooperative core documents first: bylaws, membership agreement, patronage policy, then the committee and procedure documents.

## What changed in the governance package

The cooperative-governance package now reflects these approved structural choices:
- hybrid founder-to-worker transition,
- 3-seat cooperative board,
- 2/3 member approval for worker-owner admissions,
- 2/3 member approval for expulsion after due process,
- labor-compensation-based patronage,
- narrow founder reserved matters with an automatic sunset framework,
- added committee, conflict, election, discipline, and capital-account machinery.

## Minimum professional review before use

Have counsel or a tax professional review at least the following before relying on them in live operations:
- articles and operating agreement,
- compensation model,
- privacy policy and website data practices,
- contractor IP language,
- digital product terms,
- cooperative conversion mechanics,
- patronage and tax treatment assumptions,
- the founder reserved-matters and sunset structure.

## Source-grounded legal notes

Michigan states that governance of an LLC is set by the articles or operating agreement, and Michigan law expressly recognizes operating agreements, including for single-member LLCs. Michigan also defines a “worker cooperative” in its employee-owned corporation statute, which is useful as a directional statutory reference when designing worker-owned governance. For tax administration, the IRS states that cooperatives file Form 1099-PATR for recipients of at least $10 in patronage dividends or related distributions, subject to the current instructions.
