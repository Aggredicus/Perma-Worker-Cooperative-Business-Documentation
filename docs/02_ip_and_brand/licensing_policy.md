# Licensing Policy

## Purpose

This policy explains how the Company classifies, approves, and licenses its intellectual property. It is intended to prevent accidental misuse of Company assets and to support deliberate choices between proprietary, client-restricted, shared, and open licensing models.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- documents,
- templates,
- training materials,
- software and scripts,
- visual designs,
- maps and diagrams,
- client deliverables,
- educational content,
- brand assets.

## Asset categories

### 1. Proprietary internal assets
Used internally only. No external license is granted without written approval.

Examples:
- internal systems,
- unpublished templates,
- operating manuals,
- private datasets,
- confidential workflows.

### 2. Commercial customer-licensed assets
Licensed to customers under paid terms, often nonexclusive and subject to use restrictions.

Examples:
- paid templates,
- digital planning tools,
- downloadable business kits,
- course materials.

### 3. Client-specific deliverables
Prepared for a specific client. Ownership and reuse rules depend on the client contract.

Default rule unless overridden by contract: the Company retains ownership of its background methods, templates, and know-how, while the client receives a license to use the final deliverable for the agreed project purpose.

### 4. Shared or open assets
Released under a specific open or shared license approved by the Company.

Examples:
- selected educational documents,
- public resources,
- open-source code.

## Approval authority

The following licensing decisions require written approval by authorized management:
- releasing an asset publicly,
- offering a perpetual exclusive license,
- assigning ownership to a customer,
- incorporating third-party assets with restrictive terms,
- changing a previously proprietary asset to open release.

## Standard licensing positions

1. **Brand assets:** never open-source by default.
2. **Core business templates and systems:** proprietary unless intentionally productized.
3. **Client deliverables:** license for project use; no resale or republication without permission unless contract says otherwise.
4. **Educational downloads:** governed by posted purchase terms.
5. **Software:** license selected per project, with dependencies documented.

## Third-party license compliance

Before distribution of any asset, the owner must verify:
- third-party components are identified,
- license obligations are compatible,
- attribution duties are documented,
- copyleft implications are understood,
- stock media terms allow the intended use.

## Licensing checklist

- [ ] Asset owner identified
- [ ] Asset category assigned
- [ ] Third-party dependencies reviewed
- [ ] Revenue intent identified
- [ ] Customer restrictions defined
- [ ] Attribution requirements documented
- [ ] Approval logged in decision log if material

## Records

All material outbound licenses, assignments, and public releases should be archived with:
- effective date,
- license version,
- counterparty if any,
- asset list,
- restrictions,
- approver.
