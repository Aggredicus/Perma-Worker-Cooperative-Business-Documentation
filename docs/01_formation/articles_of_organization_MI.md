# Articles of Organization (Michigan LLC) — Filing Guide and Drafting Worksheet

> This document is a preparation worksheet for completing Michigan's Articles of Organization filing. Use it together with the official state filing form and filing portal. Do not file this worksheet itself unless counsel converts it into a filing-ready document.

## State filing overview

A Michigan LLC is formed by filing Articles of Organization with the Corporations Division of the Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA). Michigan provides Form CSCL/CD-700 for a domestic LLC filing, and the state filing system also supports online submission. The state form contains only the minimum information required to make the filing acceptable and may not include business-specific protections or governance logic. See official Michigan filing materials before submission.

## Optional provisions to consider with counsel

Michigan's minimum form is intentionally simple. Depending on counsel advice, consider whether separate articles provisions, attached riders, or later amendments are advisable for:
- management authority clarity,
- limitations on member liability to the extent permitted,
- indemnification framework,
- series or protected-cell concepts if ever relevant,
- special purpose or public-benefit language if strategically useful,
- conversion readiness language if counsel believes helpful.

## Internal approvals

Before filing, obtain written internal approval of:
1. legal name,
2. principal business description,
3. registered agent,
4. organizer authority,
5. intended operating agreement,
6. initial capital plan.

## Post-filing tasks

After filing confirmation is received:
- obtain EIN,
- open business bank account,
- adopt operating agreement,
- document initial capital contributions,
- assign founder IP to the LLC,
- register tax accounts as needed,
- obtain insurance,
- update website and customer contracts with the correct legal entity name.

## Notes

Michigan indicates that Form CSCL/CD-700 may be used to draft domestic LLC articles, and Michigan's LLC materials and FAQs describe online filing through the state's CorpFile system. Filing fees and forms should always be confirmed against the current state schedule before submission.
