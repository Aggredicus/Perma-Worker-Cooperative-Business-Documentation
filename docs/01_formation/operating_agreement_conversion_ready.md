# Michigan LLC Operating Agreement (Conversion-Ready Draft)

> **Status:** Launch-stage operating agreement draft for a Michigan LLC designed to preserve a clean path toward future worker-cooperative conversion. This is a serious drafting template for attorney review, not signed legal advice.

## 1. Parties and formation

This Operating Agreement (the “Agreement”) is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** by **[Founder Name]** as the sole initial member, and by any later admitted members in accordance with this Agreement, for **[Company Name], LLC**, a Michigan limited liability company (the “Company”).

The Company was formed by filing Articles of Organization with the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs.

## 2. Purpose

The Company may engage in regenerative design, permaculture services, ecological restoration, training, educational products, software-enabled services, and any lawful business for which a Michigan LLC may be organized.

## 3. Principal office and registered office

The Company shall maintain a principal office, registered office, and registered agent as required by law.

## 4. Term

The Company shall continue until dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or law.

## 5. Member-managed company; founder control at launch

### 5.1 Initial management
The Company is initially managed by its sole member unless and until this Agreement is amended or additional members are admitted on stated terms.

### 5.2 Founder authority
Until the Company affirmatively adopts a different governance structure, the Founder has authority over:
- budgets,
- hiring and firing,
- compensation,
- contracting,
- banking,
- pricing,
- IP licensing and assignment,
- tax elections,
- strategic direction,
- all other ordinary business matters.

### 5.3 No implied worker governance before adoption
No employee, contractor, advisor, or recurring collaborator becomes a member, equity owner, or governance participant solely by performing labor, receiving compensation, or participating in advisory discussions.

## 6. Capital contributions and records

### 6.1 Initial contribution
The Founder’s initial contribution is described in the capitalization record adopted by the Company.

### 6.2 Additional contributions
No person is required to make additional capital contributions unless agreed in writing.

### 6.3 Capital records
The Company shall maintain records of contributions, distributions, and any equity or quasi-equity arrangements.

## 7. Profits, losses, and distributions

Profits and losses shall be allocated in accordance with tax law and the Company’s books. Cash distributions, if any, shall be made at the discretion of the Founder while the Company remains founder-controlled, subject to solvency and legal requirements.

## 8. Tax treatment and EIN

The Company shall obtain an EIN and maintain tax classification as determined by the Founder in consultation with tax advisors.

## 9. Admission of additional members before conversion

### 9.1 Written amendment required
No additional person may become a member except by written amendment to this Agreement or a joinder adopted by the Founder.

### 9.2 No accidental admission
The Company rejects any theory of accidental membership based solely on contribution of services, titles, oral promises, or expectations.

### 9.3 Membership classes before conversion
If additional members are admitted before formal cooperative conversion, the Founder may create limited classes or voting arrangements to preserve conversion flexibility, provided they are stated in writing.

## 10. Records and decisions

The Company shall maintain:
- a capitalization schedule,
- tax and accounting records,
- major decision log,
- minutes or written consents for material governance actions,
- IP assignments and licenses,
- policy adoption records.

## 11. Mission and governance evolution

The Company intends to evolve toward a worker-owned cooperative structure when operationally ready. That intent does not obligate immediate conversion, does not create vested worker rights before adoption, and does not override the Founder’s present authority under this Agreement.

## 12. Conversion planning commitment

The Company commits to maintain and periodically review documents supporting future cooperative conversion, including:
- governance evolution policy,
- conversion plan,
- draft bylaws,
- draft membership agreement,
- draft patronage policy,
- records sufficient to evaluate candidacy and labor compensation if conversion becomes appropriate.

## 13. Reserved matters before conversion

Until formal conversion, the following actions require Founder approval and may not be delegated except by express written instrument:
- amendment of this Agreement,
- admission of members,
- sale of substantial assets,
- transfer of core IP,
- merger, conversion, dissolution, or winding up,
- incurrence of extraordinary debt in excess of the lesser of **$50,000 or 20% of trailing 12-month gross revenue**, excluding ordinary-course trade payables not used as disguised financing,
- granting equity or profit-sharing rights that could impair future conversion design.

## 14. Conversion trigger review

The Founder shall review conversion readiness at least quarterly once the Company has either:
- three or more recurring workers besides the Founder,
- twelve months of stable operations,
- recurring profitability,
- operational dependence on non-founder labor.

The review is intended to ensure the Company does not indefinitely delay transition work after worker dependency becomes real.

## 15. Planned future cooperative governance package

If and when the Company converts, the default intended package is:
- hybrid founder-to-worker transition,
- one class of worker-owner members,
- one-member, one-vote member rights,
- three-seat board,
- one temporary Founder Director seat,
- two member-elected Worker Director seats,
- two-thirds member vote for membership admissions,
- two-thirds member vote for expulsion after due process,
- patronage based primarily on labor compensation,
- founder reserved matters that sunset automatically by milestone.

This Section is a statement of planned governance architecture and does not itself create immediate enforceable cooperative rights before conversion.

## 16. Intellectual property and confidential information

All Company-owned IP, assigned founder IP, work product, software, templates, maps, customer materials, and confidential information shall be handled under separate agreements and policies adopted by the Company.

## 17. Limitation of personal liability and indemnification

To the extent permitted by law, the Company shall indemnify the Founder and other authorized managers, officers, or agents acting in good faith on behalf of the Company, subject to exclusions for fraud, willful misconduct, or knowing legal violations.

## 18. Dissolution

The Company may be dissolved by the Founder while founder-controlled, or later by whatever approval structure is adopted in writing.

## 19. Amendments

This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by the Founder while the Company remains solely founder-controlled, or by the member approval structure later adopted in a valid amendment.
