# Worker-Owner Membership Agreement (Draft for Conversion Adoption)

> **Status:** Future-state agreement to be signed by each worker-owner upon admission to the Cooperative. This draft is aligned to the approved governance package: six-month candidacy, board recommendation plus two-thirds member approval, labor-based patronage, and a hybrid founder-to-worker transition.

This Worker-Owner Membership Agreement (the “Agreement”) is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** by and between **[Cooperative Name]** (the “Cooperative”) and **[Member Name]** (“Member”).

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to define the rights, duties, economic expectations, governance obligations, confidentiality requirements, and exit mechanics applicable to a worker-owner member of the Cooperative.

## 2. Relationship to governing documents

This Agreement supplements and incorporates by reference the Cooperative’s:
- Articles,
- Bylaws,
- Patronage Policy,
- Capital Accounts or Internal Equity Policy,
- Conflict of Interest Policy,
- Director and Governance Code of Conduct if applicable,
- Discipline and Appeals Procedure,
- other duly adopted policies.

If this Agreement conflicts with the Articles or Bylaws, the Articles and Bylaws control unless law requires otherwise.

## 3. Conditions of membership

Membership is effective only after all of the following have occurred:
1. Member completed the required candidacy period unless formally waived under an adopted policy.
2. Member completed required onboarding and governance training.
3. The Membership Committee completed its review.
4. The board recommended the Member for admission.
5. The members approved admission by at least a two-thirds (2/3) vote under the Bylaws.
6. This Agreement was signed.
7. The membership capital contribution obligation was documented.
8. The Cooperative entered the Member in the official membership records.

## 4. Core member commitments

Member agrees to:
1. perform work in good faith and with reasonable competence,
2. uphold the mission and values of the Cooperative,
3. participate in governance in good faith,
4. comply with safety, confidentiality, and professional conduct standards,
5. disclose material conflicts of interest,
6. avoid harassment, discrimination, retaliation, theft, fraud, and serious misconduct,
7. refrain from using governance rights in bad faith to obstruct ordinary operations.

## 5. One-member, one-vote rights

Subject to law and the Bylaws, Member has one vote on member matters while in good standing.

## 6. Membership capital contribution

### 6.1 Amount
Member shall contribute membership capital in the amount of **$1,000** unless modified by a later board-approved policy that applies prospectively and uniformly.

### 6.2 Payment method
The contribution shall be paid by one of the following approved methods:
- lump sum,
- payroll deduction,
- staged payment schedule of up to **12 months**,
- another board-approved method documented in writing.

### 6.3 Failure to maintain contribution schedule
If Member falls behind on an approved capital contribution schedule, the Cooperative shall provide notice and a reasonable cure period before any loss of good standing or other action, unless fraud or intentional evasion is involved.

### 6.4 Redemption on departure
The membership capital contribution is real member capital, not a fee. It is refundable in principle on departure, but it is **not** payable immediately on demand. The Cooperative may redeem all or part of the balance on a board-approved schedule that protects the Cooperative’s solvency, reserve floor, and operating stability. Any approved redemption may be reduced by lawful offsets for amounts owed by the Member to the Cooperative and may be paid in installments.

## 7. Good standing

Member remains in good standing only while the Member:
- remains an active worker unless on approved leave,
- materially complies with the Bylaws and policies,
- is not under suspension,
- remains current on any approved capital contribution schedule or has an approved deferral,
- is not subject to a final expulsion order.

## 8. Patronage and economic rights

### 8.1 General rule
Member’s economic rights are limited to those expressly provided by law, the Articles, the Bylaws, the Patronage Policy, and this Agreement. Membership does not guarantee wages above policy, guaranteed patronage, or guaranteed redemption timing.

### 8.2 Patronage basis
If patronage is declared, Member’s share shall be based primarily on **labor compensation paid or accrued during the patronage year**, subject to reserve requirements, eligibility rules, proration rules, and lawful tax treatment.

### 8.3 No passive investment return
Member understands that the Cooperative is not designed primarily to deliver a passive return on invested capital.

## 9. Governance duties and training

Member agrees to:
- attend required governance onboarding,
- review annual reports and member notices reasonably,
- participate in elections and major decisions in good faith,
- complete refresher training required by policy for fiduciary or governance competency.

## 10. Confidentiality

### 10.1 Protected information
Member shall keep confidential all nonpublic Cooperative information, including but not limited to:
- pricing methods,
- customer information,
- employee and member data,
- strategic plans,
- financial details not released to the membership generally,
- proprietary methods, software, templates, maps, or educational materials,
- legal and dispute records.

### 10.2 Permitted disclosures
Disclosure is allowed only when reasonably necessary for Cooperative duties, legally required, or expressly authorized in writing.

### 10.3 Return of materials
Upon request or separation, Member shall return or securely delete Cooperative-confidential materials as directed.

## 11. Intellectual property and work product

### 11.1 Work made for Cooperative business
To the maximum extent permitted by law, work product created by Member within the scope of Cooperative duties, using Cooperative resources, or specifically commissioned by the Cooperative belongs to the Cooperative.

### 11.2 Pre-existing materials
Member retains ownership of clearly identified pre-existing materials listed in **Schedule A**, except to the extent licensed or assigned separately in writing.

### 11.3 Further assurances
Member shall reasonably cooperate in executing documents needed to confirm Cooperative ownership of covered work product.

## 12. Conflicts of interest

Member shall promptly disclose material conflicts of interest, including outside businesses, vendor relationships, family or romantic relationships that could affect governance decisions, or personal financial interests in a transaction involving the Cooperative.

## 13. Discipline

### 13.1 Progressive process
The Cooperative ordinarily uses progressive discipline: coaching, written warning, final warning or probation, suspension, and expulsion recommendation.

### 13.2 Suspension
Suspension may be imposed by the board under the Bylaws and Discipline Procedure.

### 13.3 Expulsion
Expulsion can occur only after written notice, an opportunity to respond, completion or waiver of internal appeal, and a two-thirds (2/3) member vote under the Bylaws.

## 14. Appeals

Member shall use the internal Discipline and Appeals Procedure before resorting to outside dispute processes to the maximum extent legally enforceable.

## 15. Separation from employment and membership

### 15.1 Automatic review upon separation
If Member separates from employment, the Cooperative shall review whether membership terminates under the Bylaws.

### 15.2 Voluntary resignation
Member may resign membership by written notice.

### 15.3 Redemption or repayment of capital
Any redemption, repayment, or offset of membership capital or internal equity shall be governed by the Bylaws, the Capital Accounts or Internal Equity Policy, the financial condition of the Cooperative, and applicable law. Repayment is not required immediately upon separation if doing so would materially impair the Cooperative.

### 15.4 Setoff
To the extent permitted by law, the Cooperative may offset documented obligations owed by Member to the Cooperative against amounts otherwise payable to Member.

## 16. Non-solicitation and fair dealing

For a period of **12 months** after separation, Member agrees not to intentionally solicit Cooperative employees, worker-owner members, current clients served by the Member through the Cooperative, or active prospects materially cultivated through Cooperative confidential information, away from the Cooperative by using confidential information or active interference. This clause shall be interpreted narrowly and not to prohibit lawful competition, general advertising, or the use of general skills and relationships not protected by law.

## 17. Dispute handling

Any dispute under this Agreement shall first proceed through internal cooperative processes, then mediation if required by policy, before litigation to the extent permitted by law.

## 18. Notices

Notices under this Agreement may be delivered by hand, mail, or secure electronic method designated by policy.

## 19. Entire agreement; amendments

This Agreement may be amended only in writing or by adoption of updated form agreements approved under the Cooperative’s governance documents, provided no amendment unlawfully impairs vested rights.
