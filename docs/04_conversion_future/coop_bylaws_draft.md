# Cooperative Bylaws (Draft for Conversion Adoption)

> **Status:** Future-state governing document for adoption at the time the Company converts from an LLC into a worker cooperative or closely aligned employee-owned cooperative corporation. This draft reflects the approved governance package: hybrid founder-to-worker transition, 3-seat board, supermajority admission and expulsion votes, and labor-compensation-based patronage. It is a serious operational draft and still requires Michigan counsel review before filing or formal adoption.

## Article 1 — Name, purpose, and cooperative character

### 1.1 Name
The name of the cooperative shall be **[Cooperative Name]** (the “Cooperative”).

### 1.2 Purpose
The Cooperative exists to conduct a regenerative design, permaculture services, ecological restoration, training, installation support, and related educational or software-enabled business on a worker-owned basis.

### 1.3 Cooperative character
The Cooperative is organized so that:
- worker-owners democratically govern the enterprise,
- economic rights are tied primarily to labor patronage rather than passive capital,
- surplus may be retained for durable enterprise stewardship before patronage distributions are made,
- governance powers transition from founder-centered control to worker-owner control under a staged model rather than abruptly or symbolically.

### 1.4 Principal office and registered office
The Cooperative shall maintain a principal office, registered office, and registered agent as required by law.

## Article 2 — Membership

### 2.1 One class of membership
Except where transitional founder protections are expressly described in these bylaws or in the approved conversion plan, the Cooperative shall have one class of worker-owner membership. Each worker-owner member shall have one vote on matters submitted to members unless law or these bylaws expressly require another structure for a limited purpose.

### 2.2 Eligibility for worker-owner membership
An individual is eligible to apply for worker-owner membership only if the individual:
- is actively employed by the Cooperative or a wholly controlled operating subsidiary,
- has completed at least **six (6) continuous months** of service in a candidacy or probationary worker status unless extended for documented cause,
- is in good standing under the membership agreement and policies,
- has completed the Cooperative’s required governance, safety, confidentiality, and financial literacy onboarding,
- signs the current Worker-Owner Membership Agreement,
- commits to any required membership capital contribution or payroll deduction schedule,
- is not subject to unresolved disciplinary suspension at the time of application.

### 2.3 Candidacy period
1. A worker entering the membership pathway shall be designated a **membership candidate**.
2. Candidacy is ordinarily six (6) months.
3. The board may extend candidacy one time for up to an additional six (6) months for documented training, performance, attendance, or culture-fit concerns.
4. The purpose of candidacy is to determine readiness for worker ownership, not to create a second-class permanent labor tier.

### 2.4 Membership application package
A membership application shall include at minimum:
- written confirmation of eligibility,
- candidacy review materials,
- supervisor or peer feedback summary,
- training completion record,
- signed acknowledgement of governance duties,
- proposed membership capital contribution arrangement.

### 2.5 Membership committee recommendation
If the Membership Committee exists, it shall review applications and issue a written recommendation to the board that states whether the applicant is recommended, recommended with conditions, or not recommended.

If the Membership Committee has not yet been formed because the Cooperative is still small, the board shall designate either:
- an ad hoc admissions panel of at least two non-conflicted persons, or
- the full board acting without conflicted persons,

to perform the same review function and create a written recommendation record.

### 2.6 Board recommendation
The board shall review the application and committee report. A majority of the full board may recommend an eligible candidate to the members for admission vote. Without board recommendation, the application does not proceed to a member vote unless these bylaws are amended.

### 2.7 Admission vote
Admission as a worker-owner requires the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of the members voting at a meeting with quorum or by another lawful voting method adopted by policy.

### 2.8 Effective date of membership
Membership becomes effective only after all of the following occur:
- the admission vote passes,
- the membership agreement is signed,
- the required capital contribution commitment is documented,
- the Cooperative records the person as a member in its official books.

### 2.9 Rights of members
Each worker-owner member in good standing has the right to:
- vote on member matters,
- stand for election to the board subject to any transitional restrictions,
- receive required reports,
- participate in patronage allocations if and when declared,
- inspect records as allowed by law and policy,
- use internal appeal procedures.

### 2.10 Obligations of members
Each member shall:
- contribute labor in good faith,
- follow safety, quality, and professional conduct standards,
- maintain confidentiality,
- disclose conflicts of interest,
- participate in governance in reasonable good faith,
- avoid conduct materially harmful to the Cooperative.

### 2.11 Good standing
A member is in good standing only if the member:
- remains an active worker,
- is not suspended,
- is current on any approved capital contribution schedule unless formally deferred,
- is not subject to an unresolved expulsion recommendation,
- materially complies with bylaws and policies.

### 2.12 Loss or termination of membership
Membership terminates upon:
- voluntary resignation,
- death,
- permanent separation from employment,
- expulsion after due process,
- failure to maintain eligibility if not cured within the allowed cure period,
- dissolution of the Cooperative.

## Article 3 — Member meetings and voting

### 3.1 Annual meeting
The Cooperative shall hold an annual member meeting for elections, major reports, patronage review, governance matters, and other business.

### 3.2 Regular meetings
The board may schedule additional regular member meetings.

### 3.3 Special meetings
Special meetings may be called by:
- the board,
- the board chair,
- any two directors acting jointly,
- written petition of at least **twenty percent (20%)** of members in good standing.

### 3.4 Notice
Notice of any member meeting shall be given no fewer than ten (10) calendar days and no more than sixty (60) calendar days before the meeting, unless emergency action is authorized by law or these bylaws.

### 3.5 Quorum
A quorum for a member meeting is **sixty percent (60%)** of members in good standing, present in person or by another lawful attendance method recognized in the meeting policy.

### 3.6 Ordinary voting standard
Except where law or these bylaws require a higher threshold, member action passes by a simple majority of votes cast at a meeting with quorum.

### 3.7 Supermajority member matters
The following require an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of votes cast at a meeting with quorum unless law requires more:
- admission of a new worker-owner member,
- expulsion of a member,
- amendment of these bylaws,
- merger, consolidation, major asset sale, or dissolution recommendation,
- adoption of a material change to patronage allocation methodology,
- waiver of founder reserved matters before sunset.

### 3.8 Voting method
Unless otherwise provided, voting may occur by voice, ballot, secure written consent, or secure electronic method adopted by policy. Secret ballot shall be used for elections, membership admissions if requested by the applicant or board, and expulsion votes.

### 3.9 Meeting agenda
Meeting agendas shall identify any action items clearly enough for members to understand whether ordinary or supermajority voting will be required.

## Article 4 — Board of directors

### 4.1 Authority of the board
The board is responsible for fiduciary oversight, strategic direction, policy adoption, executive supervision, compliance oversight, financial stewardship, and any powers not reserved to members by law or these bylaws.

### 4.2 Board size and composition
The board shall consist of **three (3) directors**.

### 4.3 Transitional hybrid composition
From conversion through the sunset of founder reserved matters, the board shall be composed as follows:
- **one Founder Director seat**, initially filled by the founder during the reserved period unless the conversion plan expressly preserves a lawful successor mechanism,
- **two Worker Director seats**, elected by the members.

After the sunset event defined in Article 11, all three seats shall be elected by the members unless the members lawfully amend these bylaws.

### 4.4 Qualifications
Each director must be a member in good standing, except the Founder Director may continue under the conversion plan during the reserved-matters period even if their status is separately defined there.

### 4.5 Terms
- Worker Directors serve **two-year staggered terms** when feasible.
- The initial Worker Directors may serve one shortened and one full term so stagger can be established.
- The Founder Director serves until resignation, removal under the conversion plan, or sunset of the founder reserved-matters period, whichever occurs first.

### 4.6 Election
Worker Directors are elected by the members by plurality if more than two candidates are standing for two seats, or by majority if there is one candidate per seat. Election procedures shall be set out in the election policy.

### 4.7 Removal
A Worker Director may be removed by a majority vote of the members at a meeting with quorum, with notice that removal is on the agenda. Removal for cause may be recommended by the Governance or Membership Committee.

### 4.8 Vacancies
A vacancy in a Worker Director seat may be filled by majority vote of the remaining directors until the next member meeting, at which time members elect a replacement for the remainder of the unexpired term if required.

A vacancy in the Founder Director seat shall be handled only as provided in the conversion plan or by the automatic sunset rules in Article 11. The remaining directors may not redesignate or permanently refill the Founder Director seat on their own.

### 4.9 Quorum and board action
- Board quorum is **two (2) directors**.
- Ordinary board action passes by majority vote of directors present at a meeting with quorum.
- A board action that implicates a founder reserved matter under Article 11 is ineffective without the vote required by that Article during the reserved period.

### 4.10 Board officers
The board shall appoint at minimum a Chair, Secretary, and Treasurer or equivalent officers. Offices may be combined except that the same person should not serve as Chair and Secretary where avoidable.

### 4.11 Board meetings
The board shall meet at least quarterly and more often as necessary.

### 4.12 Board committees
The board may create standing or special committees. The Membership Committee, Finance Committee, and any Governance Committee should ordinarily be maintained once the Cooperative has at least five worker-owner members or other operational complexity that justifies them. Before that point, the board may designate ad hoc panels for admissions, appeals, or governance review if neutrality can be maintained.

## Article 5 — Reserved powers and member powers

### 5.1 Member-reserved powers
Members reserve the power to:
- elect and remove Worker Directors,
- admit and expel members,
- amend bylaws,
- approve material patronage methodology changes,
- approve dissolution, merger, or sale of substantially all assets,
- approve other matters specifically reserved by law or these bylaws.

### 5.2 Board-reserved powers
The board reserves the power to:
- hire, evaluate, and remove executive leadership,
- approve budgets,
- adopt policies,
- supervise risk and compliance,
- recommend patronage declarations,
- oversee reserve policy,
- initiate discipline procedures below expulsion.

## Article 6 — Economic rights, capital, and patronage

### 6.1 No passive ownership entitlement
No member has a right to a passive investor-style dividend merely by reason of capital contributed. Economic rights arise only under law, these bylaws, the patronage policy, approved redemption rules, and any membership capital account records.

### 6.2 Membership capital
Each member shall contribute the required membership capital amount established by board policy and approved, if necessary, by the members. Capital may be paid by lump sum, payroll deduction, or another approved method.

### 6.3 Member capital records
The Cooperative shall maintain accurate member equity, capital account, or internal equity records in accordance with its adopted accounting policy.

### 6.4 Patronage basis
Any patronage allocation shall be based primarily on **labor compensation paid or accrued during the patronage year**, subject to eligibility rules and reserve requirements in the patronage policy.

### 6.5 Reserve priority
No patronage allocation shall be made if doing so would reduce the Cooperative below the mandatory reserve floor established by policy or board-approved financial prudence standards.

### 6.6 No automatic patronage declaration
Patronage is not automatic. It must be declared through the annual surplus process after review of reserves, liabilities, debt covenants, capital needs, and law.

## Article 7 — Discipline, suspension, expulsion, and appeals

### 7.1 Progressive discipline expectation
The Cooperative shall ordinarily use progressive discipline where appropriate: coaching, written warning, final warning or probation, suspension, and expulsion recommendation.

### 7.2 Board authority for lesser discipline
The board may impose written warning, probation, or suspension according to policy. **Suspension requires a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full board**.

### 7.3 Grounds for expulsion
A member may be recommended for expulsion for:
- serious safety violations,
- material dishonesty or theft,
- repeated and uncured policy violations,
- harassment or discrimination,
- serious breach of confidentiality,
- gross neglect of duties causing material harm,
- conduct that makes continued membership untenable after documented due process.

### 7.4 Due process
Before any expulsion vote:
- the member shall receive written notice of the allegations,
- the evidence summary shall be made available subject to confidentiality constraints,
- the member shall have a meaningful opportunity to respond,
- the member may attend the hearing with a support person, subject to reasonable confidentiality and process rules,
- the board shall issue a written recommendation before the matter goes to the members.

### 7.5 Expulsion vote
Expulsion requires an affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of members voting at a meeting with quorum after completion of the internal appeal process or waiver of appeal.

### 7.6 Internal appeal
A member recommended for expulsion may file a written internal appeal within ten (10) calendar days. The appeal shall be heard under the Discipline and Appeals Procedure. The final expulsion vote may not occur until the appeal process is completed or the appeal deadline expires.

## Article 8 — Conflicts of interest and ethics

### 8.1 Disclosure
Directors, officers, committee members, and members acting in governance roles must disclose material conflicts of interest promptly.

### 8.2 Recusal
A conflicted decision-maker shall not vote on a matter in which they have a material personal interest, except where counsel advises otherwise and disclosure is made.

### 8.3 Code of conduct
The Cooperative shall maintain a director and governance code of conduct covering confidentiality, fiduciary care, respectful process, and anti-retaliation expectations.

## Article 9 — Deadlock and dispute management

### 9.1 Board deadlock
If the board deadlocks on a material operational matter for two consecutive duly noticed meetings, the board shall use the deadlock process in the Board Charter or a board-approved mediation procedure.

### 9.2 Escalation
For non-reserved matters, unresolved deadlocks may be referred to member advisory discussion, outside facilitation, or a later board meeting with revised proposals. Deadlock does not automatically transfer ordinary board powers to the membership unless these bylaws or law so provide.

### 9.3 Emergency authority
If immediate action is reasonably necessary to prevent serious legal, financial, safety, or operational harm and the board cannot convene in time, designated officers may act within the limits of the Emergency Authority Policy and must promptly report the action to the board.

## Article 10 — Records, notices, and fiscal year

### 10.1 Records
The Cooperative shall maintain accurate records of member rosters, meeting minutes, board actions, policies, capital records, patronage records, and required filings.

### 10.2 Fiscal year
The board shall establish the fiscal year.

### 10.3 Notices and consent to electronic delivery
Members may consent to receiving notices and reports by secure electronic means.

## Article 11 — Founder reserved matters and sunset

### 11.1 Hybrid transition principle
The Cooperative adopts a hybrid founder-to-worker governance structure for a limited transition period in order to protect continuity, existing mission-critical IP, financial solvency, and enterprise viability while democratic worker governance matures.

### 11.2 Founder reserved matters during the reserved period
Until sunset under Section 11.4, the following actions require approval by the Founder Director in addition to any other required board or member vote:
- amendment of the mission statement in a way that materially changes the regenerative and worker-ownership character of the enterprise,
- sale, exclusive license, or encumbrance of core founder-contributed IP identified in the conversion plan,
- merger, dissolution, or sale of substantially all assets,
- incurrence of extraordinary debt in excess of **the lesser of $50,000 or 20% of trailing 12-month gross revenue**, excluding ordinary-course trade payables not used as disguised financing,
- amendment of these bylaws in a way that materially impairs the conversion protections promised in the approved conversion plan,
- removal of the Founder Director seat before sunset.

### 11.3 Narrow construction
Founder reserved matters shall be construed narrowly. They do not create a general founder veto over ordinary operations, hiring, scheduling, budgets within approved ranges, member admissions, or patronage decisions consistent with these bylaws.

### 11.4 Sunset event
Founder reserved matters and the Founder Director seat sunset automatically upon the earliest of:
- **twenty-four (24) months** after the legal conversion effective date,
- the Cooperative reaching **five (5) worker-owner members in good standing** for at least ninety (90) consecutive days,
- a two-thirds (2/3) member vote and consent of the Founder Director to early sunset,
- resignation or permanent incapacity of the Founder Director if no lawful successor right is expressly preserved in the conversion plan.

### 11.5 Post-sunset governance
After sunset, all board seats shall be elected by the members and founder reserved matters shall terminate unless lawfully replaced by a member-approved bylaw amendment.

## Article 12 — Amendments

These bylaws may be amended only by the affirmative vote of at least **two-thirds (2/3)** of members voting at a meeting with quorum, except where a higher threshold is required by law. During the founder reserved period, amendments affecting Article 11 also require Founder Director approval.
