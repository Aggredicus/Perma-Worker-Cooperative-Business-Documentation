# Cooperative Corporation Conversion Plan

## Purpose

This plan describes the staged path for converting the Company from a Michigan LLC into a worker-owned cooperative or closely aligned employee-owned structure.

This version reflects the approved constitutional package:
- hybrid founder-to-worker transition,
- 3-seat board,
- 2/3 member approval for worker-owner admission,
- 2/3 member approval for expulsion after due process,
- patronage based primarily on labor compensation.

## Why conversion is deferred at launch

The business is expected to begin as a founder-led LLC because that structure is administratively simpler during formation. Conversion is deferred so the business can first build:
- reliable bookkeeping,
- stable pricing and revenue,
- documented operations,
- staff training systems,
- legal and tax readiness,
- a credible worker-owner pathway.

## Conversion goals

1. Protect the mission while broadening ownership.
2. Reward sustained worker contribution.
3. Build durable governance rather than symbolic participation.
4. Avoid tax, governance, or capital-table chaos.
5. Preserve critical IP and brand ownership continuity.
6. Transition founder protections into democratic governance on a clear sunset schedule.

## Preconditions for active conversion

All or most of the following should be met before moving from study to execution:
- at least **12 months** of orderly bookkeeping,
- at least **6–12 months** of stable operations with recurring workers,
- no unresolved major tax filings or legal defects,
- clearly documented job roles and evaluation processes,
- written worker-owner candidacy criteria,
- counsel and CPA engaged on the conversion,
- clear plan for existing founder interests and future member economics,
- compensation records clean enough to support labor-compensation-based patronage accounting.

## Approved target governance design

The intended target design at conversion is:
- one class of worker-owner members,
- one-member, one-vote member rights,
- three-seat board,
- one Founder Director seat during a limited hybrid period,
- two Worker Director seats elected by members,
- founder reserved matters limited to existential or constitutional topics,
- founder reserved matters sunset at the earliest of 24 months post-conversion, five worker-owner members maintained for 90 days, earlier consensual sunset, or automatic sunset upon founder resignation/incapacity absent an express lawful successor instrument,
- all board seats member-elected after sunset.

## Founder-interest conversion parameters to carry into execution

The final conversion package should preserve the following approved parameters unless changed by later documented decision and counsel review:
- Protected founder-contributed IP during the reserved period should include a moderate schedule covering core software and code repositories, brand marks, proprietary templates and map/design systems, documented methods and operating frameworks, and premium training or curriculum content identified on an attached schedule.
- Founder-specific economic rights, if any, should be transitional rather than permanent and should be documented separately from ordinary worker-owner patronage rights.
- There is **no successor right** to the Founder Director seat unless an express written successor instrument is adopted before the founder’s resignation, removal, or incapacity and is lawful under the final entity structure.
- “Extraordinary debt” for founder reserved-matter approval means debt in excess of **the lesser of $50,000 or 20% of trailing 12-month gross revenue**, excluding ordinary-course trade payables not used as disguised financing.

## Workstreams

### 1. Legal workstream
- confirm the Michigan statutory conversion path,
- choose the exact target cooperative entity form,
- finalize articles, bylaws, membership agreement, and board documents,
- map how LLC interests, contracts, permits, and registrations move into the new entity,
- prepare all required member, board, and filing approvals.

### 2. Tax and accounting workstream
- assess tax consequences of conversion,
- finalize the labor-compensation patronage model,
- determine treatment of retained earnings and reserves,
- implement member capital tracking,
- prepare annual patronage reporting workflow and Form 1099-PATR handling if required.

### 3. Governance workstream
- finalize worker-owner candidacy criteria,
- train workers on cooperative governance and fiduciary duties,
- install election procedures,
- adopt conflict, appeals, and discipline procedures,
- define board calendar and committee cadence.

### 4. Operations workstream
- document roles, onboarding, and evaluation,
- align compensation systems with patronage integrity,
- prepare worker communications,
- implement recordkeeping controls,
- define how current customers and partners will be notified or not notified, depending on legal need.

## Suggested timeline

| Phase | Duration | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Readiness assessment | 1–2 months | Go / no-go memo |
| Professional structuring | 1–3 months | Legal and tax design |
| Drafting and education | 1–2 months | Final governance package |
| Approval and filing | 1 month | Conversion documents filed |
| Post-conversion stabilization | 3–6 months | Training, corrections, and reserve discipline |

## Approval path

1. Founder and management prepare readiness memo.
2. The LLC authorizes launch of the formal conversion process.
3. Counsel and CPA finalize entity, tax, and filing mechanics.
4. Workers who will become initial members review the governance package.
5. The required LLC approvals are obtained.
6. Required state filings are submitted.
7. Cooperative governance documents take effect.
8. Initial board is seated under the hybrid model.

## Initial post-conversion priorities

During the first 90 days after conversion, the Cooperative should:
- train the board,
- test meeting cadence and quorum discipline,
- confirm capital account or internal equity records,
- confirm the reserve floor,
- run a mock patronage worksheet,
- document founder reserved matters in a concise board reference memo,
- review discipline and appeals procedures before a live dispute forces improvisation.

## Founder-protected IP schedule categories

Before conversion closing, attach a concise schedule identifying the specific founder-contributed assets that count as protected core IP during the reserved period. Unless narrowed by counsel, that schedule should use the following categories:
- core software, source code repositories, deployment scripts, and technical architecture authored or controlled by the founder before conversion,
- brand names, logos, marks, and distinctive trade dress associated with the enterprise,
- proprietary templates, map editors, design systems, and client-delivery frameworks,
- documented methods, operating frameworks, and internal training systems developed by the founder,
- premium course, curriculum, and training content designated as founder-contributed content before conversion.

The schedule should be narrow enough to avoid creating a general founder veto over ordinary operations.

## Review cadence before conversion

This plan should be reviewed quarterly and updated in the decision log.
