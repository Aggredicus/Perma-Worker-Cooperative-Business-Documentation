# Patronage Dividend Policy (Draft for Conversion Adoption)

> **Status:** Future-state policy for use only after legal conversion and after review by a cooperative-savvy CPA and counsel. This draft operationalizes the approved choice that patronage is based primarily on labor compensation rather than hours.

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a transparent and repeatable method for determining whether annual net surplus may be allocated to worker-owner members as patronage, how reserve protections apply first, and how allocations are calculated, documented, and reported.

## 2. Guiding principles

The Cooperative adopts the following patronage principles:
- labor, not passive capital, is the primary basis for member economic participation,
- the enterprise must remain solvent and adequately capitalized before distributions are declared,
- formulas must be simple enough to administer and explain,
- calculations must be auditable,
- tax reporting must be handled in accordance with professional advice and applicable law.

## 3. Definitions

For this policy:
- **Patronage Year** means the Cooperative’s fiscal year unless the board adopts another annual allocation period.
- **Eligible Labor Compensation** means gross labor compensation paid or accrued for services to the Cooperative during the Patronage Year, subject to exclusions below.
- **Patronage Pool** means the amount, if any, approved for allocation after the reserve waterfall in Section 5.
- **Good Standing** has the meaning assigned in the Bylaws and Membership Agreement.

## 4. Eligibility

A person is eligible to share in a patronage allocation only if the person:
- was a worker-owner member in good standing for at least part of the Patronage Year,
- performed labor services for the Cooperative during the Patronage Year,
- is not finally expelled before the declaration date unless the board determines that prorated eligibility remains appropriate under law and policy,
- has any required tax forms and current contact information on file.

## 5. Annual surplus waterfall

Before any patronage declaration, annual net surplus shall be applied in the following order unless law, debt covenants, or professional advice require another order:
1. payment or accrual of taxes, payroll obligations, and legally required liabilities,
2. cure of any overdue obligations that materially threaten solvency or compliance,
3. maintenance of the mandatory operating reserve floor,
4. board-approved strategic reserves for equipment, software, vehicles, restoration capacity, seasonal volatility, or other board-documented needs,
5. debt service and board-approved capital expenditures already committed,
6. the remaining amount, if any, may become the Patronage Pool.

## 6. Mandatory reserve floor

### 6.1 Reserve target
The Cooperative shall maintain a minimum operating reserve target equal to **three (3) months of average core operating expenses**, unless the board and members later adopt a higher floor.

### 6.2 Effect of reserve shortfall
No patronage shall be declared if the declaration would reduce unrestricted cash or equivalent operating liquidity below the reserve floor, unless professional advice confirms a narrower solvency-safe exception and the members approve it by supermajority.

## 7. Patronage formula

### 7.1 Primary metric
The Cooperative shall calculate patronage primarily on **Eligible Labor Compensation** during the Patronage Year.

### 7.2 Included compensation
Eligible Labor Compensation ordinarily includes:
- regular wages or salary,
- overtime,
- production or project pay,
- taxable bonuses tied to labor performed,
- other board-approved compensation categories tied directly to labor.

### 7.3 Excluded compensation
Unless specifically approved in advance and documented consistently, Eligible Labor Compensation excludes:
- reimbursements,
- severance,
- non-labor capital repayments,
- prior-year corrections not attributable to the Patronage Year,
- discretionary founder-specific transition payments not intended as labor compensation,
- any category excluded by CPA guidance for patronage consistency.

### 7.4 Calculation method
Each eligible member’s patronage share is:

**Member Eligible Labor Compensation / Total Eligible Labor Compensation of all eligible members × Patronage Pool**

### 7.5 Proration
If a member joins or exits during the year, only Eligible Labor Compensation earned while eligible and in good standing during the relevant period shall count, unless law or CPA guidance requires a different rule.

## 8. Board recommendation and member review

### 8.1 Board role
The board shall review annual results and determine whether any Patronage Pool exists after required reserves and obligations.

### 8.2 Finance Committee review
If the Finance Committee exists, it shall review the proposed calculation worksheet, exclusions, and reserve analysis before the board recommends any declaration.

### 8.3 Member report
Before any patronage declaration vote required by the governing documents, members shall receive a report that includes:
- annual net surplus,
- reserve status,
- Patronage Pool proposed,
- total Eligible Labor Compensation,
- each member’s percentage share and proposed amount,
- expected cash and retained portions,
- tax reporting assumptions.

## 9. Form of allocation

### 9.1 Cash and retained portions
The Cooperative may allocate patronage partly in cash and partly as retained or written-notice capital, subject to law and professional advice.

### 9.2 Default approach
Until the Cooperative has mature cooperative tax administration in place, the default board recommendation should be conservative and may favor larger retained portions over cash distributions in order to protect reserves.

## 10. Loss years and low-surplus years

If the Cooperative has no allocable Patronage Pool, no patronage is required. The board shall still report the result to the members. The Cooperative may instead prioritize reserves, debt reduction, and capital stabilization.

## 11. Anti-manipulation rules

### 11.1 Compensation integrity
Compensation arrangements shall not be altered late in the Patronage Year primarily to distort patronage outcomes.

### 11.2 Related-party review
Compensation for founders, directors, officers, or related parties shall be reviewed under conflict-of-interest procedures if changes may materially affect patronage allocations.

## 12. Tax reporting and forms

The Cooperative shall issue required tax documentation and reporting with the assistance of qualified tax professionals. The IRS instructs cooperatives to file Form 1099-PATR for each person to whom the cooperative paid at least $10 in patronage dividends or related distributions, subject to the form instructions in effect for the relevant tax year.

## 13. Disputed calculations

A member may request a calculation review within fifteen (15) calendar days after receiving the annual patronage report. The Finance Committee or board shall issue a written response. Minor errors may be corrected administratively; material errors shall be documented in board minutes.

## 14. Policy amendment

Because patronage methodology affects core economic rights, any material amendment to this policy’s formula or reserve priority shall require the bylaw-level approval required for major patronage changes.
