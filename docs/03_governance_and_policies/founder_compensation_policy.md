# Founder Compensation Policy

## Purpose

This policy creates disciplined rules for compensating founder labor during the launch and scaling phases of the business. It is intended to reduce ambiguity, protect cash flow, support clean bookkeeping, and avoid casual mixing of payroll, draws, reimbursements, and ownership economics.

## Policy principles

1. The Company should pay sustainably, not aspirationally.
2. Compensation decisions must reflect cash flow, tax treatment, role value, and business stage.
3. Founder sacrifice may occur, but should be visible, documented, and periodically reviewed.
4. Ownership does not automatically justify undocumented withdrawals.
5. Future worker-owner fairness should be considered even at the founder stage.

## Compensation channels

The Company may compensate a founder through one or more of the following, subject to tax classification and professional advice:
- payroll wages,
- owner draw,
- guaranteed payment,
- approved reimbursement,
- deferred compensation memorialized in writing,
- approved bonus tied to performance or milestones.

## Launch-stage rules

During the launch phase, the Company should adopt a written monthly founder compensation plan stating:
- base target amount,
- method of payment,
- timing,
- whether unpaid labor is being intentionally deferred or simply not compensated,
- reimbursement treatment,
- review date.

## Expense reimbursements

A founder may be reimbursed only for legitimate business expenses supported by receipts or equivalent records and submitted within [30] days unless an exception is approved.

## Compensation review triggers

Compensation should be reviewed when any of the following occur:
- monthly revenue changes by more than [20]%,
- payroll begins for other workers,
- the Company elects S corporation taxation,
- a major financing event occurs,
- conversion planning accelerates,
- persistent underpayment creates founder hardship or tax confusion.

## Prohibited practices

The following are prohibited unless separately documented and professionally reviewed:
- treating personal spending as business spending,
- taking irregular undocumented cash withdrawals,
- changing compensation method without accounting review,
- promising future ownership in place of wages without legal documentation,
- backfilling missing records from memory months later without notation.

## Annual review questions

1. Is founder pay sustainable relative to margin?
2. Are reimbursements being used to mask undercompensation?
3. Is the current method tax-appropriate?
4. Is the founder carrying too much unpaid labor?
5. Does the structure remain fair if future worker-owners are added?
