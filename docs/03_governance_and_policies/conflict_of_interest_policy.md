# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Purpose

This policy protects the Company and future Cooperative from decisions distorted by undisclosed personal interests.

## Covered persons

This policy applies to members, directors, officers, committee members, and anyone participating materially in governance decisions.

## Conflicts requiring disclosure

Examples include:
- ownership in a vendor or customer,
- close family or romantic relationship with a person affected by the decision,
- personal financial gain from a transaction,
- side businesses competing with or materially overlapping the enterprise,
- compensation changes that materially affect one’s own patronage outcome.

## Disclosure rule

A covered person shall disclose the conflict before discussion or vote whenever reasonably possible.

## Recusal

A person with a material conflict shall not vote on the matter and should leave deliberation if appropriate, unless the board determines that limited factual participation is necessary.

## Documentation

Minutes shall reflect the disclosure and recusal.

## Violations

Failure to disclose a material conflict may be grounds for discipline and may invalidate a decision if the omission was material.
