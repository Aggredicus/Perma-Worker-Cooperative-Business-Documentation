# Recordkeeping and Minutes Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes minimum standards for preserving the Company's key legal, financial, operational, and governance records.

## Objectives

1. Preserve evidence of major decisions.
2. Maintain records suitable for audits, disputes, due diligence, grant review, financing, or cooperative conversion.
3. Prevent loss of critical information due to informal operations.

## Record categories

### A. Permanent records
Retain permanently unless counsel advises otherwise:
- formation documents,
- operating agreement and amendments,
- filed state documents,
- EIN confirmation,
- ownership records,
- IP assignments,
- trademark and domain records,
- major contracts,
- written consents,
- member and board resolutions,
- minutes,
- tax elections,
- conversion documents.

### B. Long-term operational records
Retain for at least the period advised by counsel/CPA and applicable law:
- tax returns and supporting workpapers,
- payroll records,
- contractor records,
- invoices and receipts,
- bank statements,
- insurance policies and claims,
- employee injury or incident reports,
- customer contracts and scopes.

### C. Working records
Retain according to operational value and legal need:
- draft proposals,
- internal planning notes,
- project files,
- training records,
- vendor correspondence.

## Minutes standard

For each formal meeting or written consent, record at minimum:
- date and time,
- participants,
- matters considered,
- decisions made,
- approvals and vote thresholds if applicable,
- follow-up actions,
- document links.

## Storage controls

1. Signed records should be stored in a secure folder with limited edit access.
2. The repo may contain editable source versions, but signed execution copies should be archived separately.
3. File naming should include effective date and version where practical.
4. Critical records should be backed up in at least two locations.

## Destruction holds

No relevant records may be destroyed if litigation, tax examination, insurance claims, or formal disputes are pending or reasonably anticipated.
