# Governance Evolution Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how the Company intends to evolve from founder-led LLC governance toward a participatory and eventually worker-owned governance model. It exists to prevent governance confusion, premature democratization without structure, and delayed transition after the business is already operationally dependent on workers.

This policy now reflects the approved governance package:
- a **hybrid founder-to-worker transition model**,
- a **3-seat cooperative board** at conversion,
- **two-thirds member approval** for worker-owner admissions,
- **two-thirds member approval** for expulsion after due process,
- patronage based primarily on **labor compensation**.

## Governance stages

### Stage 0 — Founder launch
Characteristics:
- one founder or a very small founding group,
- limited staff,
- founder-managed decisions,
- simple recordkeeping and rapid iteration.

Primary needs:
- core legal compliance,
- strong financial discipline,
- clean IP ownership,
- customer terms and risk controls.

### Stage 1 — Managed team buildout
Characteristics:
- recurring contractors or first employees,
- defined roles and basic supervision,
- regular meetings and operating policies.

Primary needs:
- role clarity,
- compensation consistency,
- records discipline,
- onboarding and performance expectations.

### Stage 2 — Participatory pre-cooperative structure
Characteristics:
- multiple regular workers,
- recurring profitability or stable revenue,
- documented training and review systems,
- growing importance of worker voice.

Primary needs:
- advisory councils or committees,
- transparent role ladders,
- ownership eligibility criteria,
- feasibility work for cooperative conversion.

### Stage 3 — Formal worker-owner conversion
Characteristics:
- legal conversion or equivalent adoption,
- worker-owner admission process,
- patronage or member-based economic rights,
- 3-seat board governance,
- founder transition protections with defined sunset.

Primary needs:
- bylaws,
- membership agreement,
- patronage accounting,
- board training,
- dispute resolution,
- transition milestone tracking.

## Readiness indicators for moving stages

The Company should review stage transition at least quarterly using the decision log. Movement between stages should be based on real readiness, not aspiration alone.

### Stage 0 to Stage 1 indicators
- consistent bookkeeping system is running,
- service offerings and pricing are documented,
- founder IP is assigned or licensed into the LLC,
- basic customer and privacy policies exist,
- the Company has a repeatable operating rhythm.

### Stage 1 to Stage 2 indicators
- at least 2 recurring workers or equivalent regular contributors,
- defined job roles and compensation bands,
- onboarding and safety materials in place,
- minutes and records are being maintained,
- first participatory review or advisory process established.

### Stage 2 to Stage 3 indicators
All or most of the following should be met:
- at least 12 months of orderly bookkeeping,
- at least 6–12 months of stable operations with recurring workers,
- no unresolved major filing defects,
- worker-owner candidacy pathway documented,
- governance training materials prepared,
- counsel and CPA engaged,
- conversion documents substantially complete,
- the founder has explicitly approved the staged hybrid conversion package.

## Approved future-state governance model

When the Company converts, the default governance model shall be:
- one class of worker-owner members,
- one-member, one-vote member governance,
- a **3-seat board**,
- **1 Founder Director seat** during the temporary hybrid period,
- **2 Worker Director seats** elected by members,
- founder reserved matters defined narrowly and sunset by milestone,
- all three seats elected by members after the reserved period ends.

## Founder reserved matters philosophy

Founder protections are allowed only to preserve continuity during transition and shall be interpreted narrowly. They are not intended to preserve indefinite founder dominance over ordinary operations. Reserved matters should be limited to existential issues such as mission drift, core IP transfer, major debt, dissolution, or structural constitutional changes.

## Member admission philosophy

Worker ownership should be meaningful rather than symbolic. Therefore the Company adopts the following principles for future membership admissions:
- candidacy is ordinarily six months,
- governance and safety training are required,
- the board must recommend the applicant,
- admission requires **two-thirds member approval**,
- admission standards should be documented and consistently applied,
- candidacy should not become a permanent second-tier labor status.

## Discipline and expulsion philosophy

The Company will use progressive discipline where possible. Worker ownership should not be revoked casually. Future expulsion standards should therefore include:
- written notice,
- opportunity to respond,
- internal appeal,
- a board recommendation,
- final expulsion only by **two-thirds member approval**.

## Economic transition philosophy

The Company’s intended economic structure after conversion is:
- capital contributions may be required for membership,
- passive investment return is not the primary economic logic,
- reserves are protected before distributions,
- patronage is based primarily on **labor compensation**,
- compensation systems should not be manipulated to distort patronage.

## Operational actions for the current LLC

Before conversion, the LLC should:
- maintain high-quality payroll and compensation records,
- define clear worker roles,
- test advisory participation without creating shadow governance,
- document candidacy and review practices,
- preserve clean founder-to-company IP chains,
- keep board-style minutes even if not legally required at every stage,
- maintain reserve discipline to prepare for future patronage accounting.

## Review cadence

This policy should be reviewed quarterly while the Company remains an LLC and again immediately before any formal conversion vote.
