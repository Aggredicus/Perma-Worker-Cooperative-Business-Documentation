# Emergency Authority Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines limited emergency action authority when serious harm is likely and the board cannot convene in time.

## Emergency standard

Emergency authority may be used only when delay would likely create serious legal, financial, safety, compliance, or operational harm and ordinary board process is not reasonably available in time.

## Authorized actors

The board may designate specific officers or managers who may act under this policy. No person has emergency authority merely by title unless designated in writing.

## Limits

Emergency authority does not permit:
- amendment of bylaws,
- admission or expulsion of members,
- sale of substantially all assets,
- action on founder reserved matters without the required approvals,
- any step more irreversible than reasonably necessary to stabilize the situation.

## Decision rule

The authorized actor should choose the least irreversible, lowest-risk action reasonably available under the circumstances.

## Reporting

Any emergency action must be reported to the board as soon as practicable, with:
- the nature of the emergency,
- the action taken,
- alternatives considered,
- any financial or legal consequences,
- any follow-up action required.

## Ratification

The board should review, ratify, modify, or unwind the emergency action at the earliest practicable meeting.
