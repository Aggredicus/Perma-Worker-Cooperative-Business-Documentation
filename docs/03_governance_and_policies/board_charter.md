# Board Charter

> **Use:** Future-state cooperative board operating charter. This should be adopted with the bylaws or immediately after conversion.

## Purpose

This charter defines the board’s operating rhythm, authority boundaries, deadlock handling, officer expectations, and meeting discipline.

## Core board role

The board is responsible for:
- strategy and mission stewardship,
- executive oversight,
- budget approval and financial monitoring,
- reserve discipline,
- policy adoption,
- legal and fiduciary oversight,
- recommendation of patronage declarations,
- oversight of risk, culture, and member-process integrity.

## Board composition

The board consists of three directors under the bylaws:
- one Founder Director during the temporary hybrid period,
- two Worker Directors elected by the members.

## Matters the board decides

Unless reserved to members, the board decides:
- annual budgets,
- hiring and firing of top management,
- compensation frameworks,
- policy adoption,
- committee appointments,
- financing proposals within approved limits,
- operational priorities.

## Matters the board does not decide alone

The board may not unilaterally decide matters reserved to members or founder reserved matters without the approvals required by the bylaws.

## Meeting cadence

The board shall meet at least quarterly. A monthly meeting rhythm is preferred during the first year after conversion.

## Standard board agenda

1. Call to order and quorum confirmation
2. Conflict disclosures
3. Approval of prior minutes
4. Financial review
5. Operations update
6. Membership and people matters
7. Strategic or policy decisions
8. Reserved matters review if any
9. Executive session if needed
10. Action summary and adjournment

## Deadlock procedure

If the board deadlocks on a material issue for two consecutive meetings:
1. the chair records the exact unresolved issue,
2. each side submits a written one-page rationale,
3. the board schedules facilitated discussion,
4. if still unresolved, the board may request nonbinding member advisory input,
5. if still unresolved and non-emergency, the item is deferred or narrowed,
6. if emergency, the least irreversible option should be preferred pending full resolution.

## Executive session

The board may enter executive session for personnel, litigation, investigation, confidential financing, or other legally sensitive matters.

## Minutes discipline

The Secretary shall record action-oriented minutes reflecting quorum, motions, votes, recusals, and key rationale without unnecessary narrative risk.

## Annual self-review

The board should conduct an annual self-review covering meeting discipline, strategic clarity, conflict handling, and founder-sunset readiness.
