# Governance Simulation Gap Memo

> Purpose: record a post-revision boardroom stress test of the cooperative governance package and identify any remaining operational gaps or redline recommendations.

## Summary judgment

The package now performs substantially better as a live governance system than the prior draft. It can support real meetings involving member admissions, discipline, surplus allocation, and a hybrid founder-to-worker transition.

The remaining weaknesses are no longer constitutional failures so much as **implementation edges**:
- small-cooperative committee availability,
- emergency authority not previously documented,
- governance-review function not previously documented,
- a few remaining business choices that should be fixed before live adoption.

## Scenario 1 — Worker-owner admission vote

### Scenario
A sixth-month candidate applies for membership. The cooperative has only three current worker-owners and no formal Membership Committee yet.

### Result
The revised package now works because the bylaws allow an ad hoc admissions panel or the non-conflicted board to generate the recommendation record.

### Remaining note
The board should adopt a simple candidacy review form before the first live admission vote.

## Scenario 2 — Member expulsion after safety violations

### Scenario
A member repeatedly violates safety rules, is suspended, appeals, and then faces a member vote on expulsion.

### Result
The documents support a complete path: progressive discipline, board suspension threshold, written notice, hearing, appeal, and 2/3 member vote.

### Remaining note
For very small co-ops, neutrality of the appeal panel may be difficult. Outside facilitation should be budgeted for serious disputes.

## Scenario 3 — Patronage declaration after a profitable year

### Scenario
The cooperative has positive annual surplus and members want cash distributions.

### Result
The package now performs credibly: reserve-first waterfall, labor-compensation formula, eligibility rules, finance review, and reporting logic are all present.

### Remaining note
The exact treatment of retained patronage, written notices of allocation, and year-end tax workflow still require CPA finalization before live use.

## Scenario 4 — Board deadlock

### Scenario
The three-seat board splits on a major compensation-framework change after one director recuses due to conflict.

### Result
The package avoids accidental overreach: with recusal and only two voting directors, ordinary action requires the approvals actually required by the bylaws, and the board charter now instructs the minutes to record deferral, temporary replacement action, or neutral mediation.

### Remaining note
Before launch, the board should pre-approve one neutral outside mediator or facilitator.

## Scenario 5 — Founder reserved matter dispute

### Scenario
Worker Directors want to license core founder-contributed software on exclusive terms during the hybrid period.

### Result
The bylaws correctly classify this as a founder reserved matter and block action without Founder Director approval.

### Remaining note
The conversion plan should attach a schedule identifying which founder-contributed IP actually counts as “core founder-contributed IP” so the reserved-matter boundary is concrete.

## Scenario 6 — Founder Director vacancy

### Scenario
The Founder Director resigns unexpectedly during the reserved period.

### Result
The revised bylaws now prevent the remaining directors from informally re-creating or refilling that seat without conversion-plan authority.

### Remaining note
The conversion plan should explicitly decide whether there is any successor right or whether resignation triggers immediate sunset.

## Scenario 7 — Emergency compliance event

### Scenario
A regulator requires immediate operational suspension of a service line before the board can convene.

### Result
This was previously under-documented. The new Emergency Authority Policy closes the gap by allowing limited, least-irreversible emergency action followed by reporting and ratification.

## Remaining items requiring deliberate approval or professional review

1. Membership capital amount and payment schedule.
2. Final non-solicitation period in the worker-owner membership agreement.
3. Exact extraordinary-debt threshold for founder reserved matters.
4. Specific list of founder-contributed IP covered during the reserved period.
5. Final cooperative entity and filing path under Michigan law.
6. CPA-reviewed patronage reporting workflow.

## Redline recommendations for the next pass

1. Add a one-page candidate scorecard as an appendix or template.
2. Add a director/officer designation memo naming who holds emergency authority.
3. Attach a schedule of founder-contributed core IP to the conversion plan.
4. Fix the worker-owner agreement’s bracketed non-solicitation duration.
5. Add one mediation/facilitation vendor selection note to board startup materials.

## Practical conclusion

After these revisions, the repo is much closer to a **usable cooperative governance package** rather than only a planning kit. The remaining issues are specific implementation choices and legal/tax finalization items, not major structural breakdowns.
