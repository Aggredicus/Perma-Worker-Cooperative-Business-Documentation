# Assumptions

## Purpose of this document

This document states the assumptions used throughout the repository so that every document can be interpreted consistently. If an assumption changes, update this file first and then revise any dependent documents identified in the cross-reference section.

## Core entity assumptions

1. **Jurisdiction.** The business is formed and primarily governed in Michigan.
2. **Initial entity type.** The business launches as a Michigan limited liability company.
3. **Management structure.** The LLC begins as either a member-managed or manager-managed entity as selected in the operating agreement, but in either case is designed to support future democratic worker governance.
4. **Future path.** The company intends to convert to a worker-owned cooperative or functionally employee-owned cooperative structure once staffing, revenue stability, and administrative maturity justify conversion.
5. **Single-founder launch.** The initial launch may involve one founder before additional members, managers, employees, or worker-owners are admitted.

## Business model assumptions

1. The company may sell a mix of:
   - permaculture design services,
   - installation planning and implementation support,
   - land assessment and mapping,
   - ecological restoration consulting,
   - educational programming, workshops, and cohort training,
   - software, templates, and digital documents,
   - branded media, written materials, and intellectual property licensing.
2. The company may operate both field-service and digital-service lines.
3. Customer work may include custom deliverables, requiring strong IP ownership rules and customer terms.
4. The company may engage employees, contractors, and future worker-owners.

## Governance assumptions

1. Major decisions should be documented even when not legally required.
2. Records should be maintained as if the business may later undergo due diligence, financing review, tax audit, grant review, or cooperative conversion scrutiny.
3. Any governance powers not expressly delegated remain with the members under the operating agreement and applicable law.
4. The company should not rely on informal verbal side deals for ownership, compensation, equity-like interests, or control rights.

## Compensation assumptions

1. Founder compensation may start below market while the company is capital constrained.
2. Owner draws, guaranteed payments, payroll wages, and reimbursements must be kept distinct in bookkeeping.
3. Equity-like economics for future worker-owners are deferred until the cooperative conversion phase unless explicitly adopted earlier by written amendment.

## Tax and compliance assumptions

1. The business will obtain an EIN only after legal formation.
2. The federal and state tax classification should be reviewed annually with a CPA.
3. Payroll registration, sales tax, local permits, insurance, and labor compliance must be handled according to actual operations and locations.
4. Nothing in this repo assumes nonprofit status.

## Intellectual property assumptions

1. IP created by the founder before or outside the company is not automatically owned by the LLC unless assigned in writing.
2. Company-funded work product should be owned by the company to the maximum extent allowed by law.
3. Open-source, shared, and proprietary assets may coexist, but each must be tracked explicitly.
4. Trademark use should be documented before substantial brand expansion.

## Privacy and data assumptions

1. The website may collect at least basic inquiry data such as name, email, phone, address, and project details.
2. The company may later use analytics, email marketing, forms, maps, uploaded files, CRM tools, and payment processors.
3. Privacy disclosures must be updated whenever data practices materially change.

## Conversion assumptions

1. Cooperative conversion is a planned future event, not an immediate legal status.
2. Conversion should only occur after the company has stable accounting, mature records, written role definitions, and a realistic member pipeline.
3. Patronage allocations should not be used until the business has formal legal and accounting support for them.

## Drafting assumptions

1. All documents are intended to be practical operating drafts.
2. Bracketed fields are placeholders that require completion.
3. Where a document references an exhibit not yet created, that exhibit should be created before operational use.
