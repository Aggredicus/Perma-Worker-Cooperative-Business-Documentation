## Mission

The Company exists to provide permaculture, ecological design, land stewardship, and regenerative business services that improve land health, operational resilience, and long-term community wealth while building a pathway to dignified worker ownership.

## Vision

To become a durable regional enterprise that combines high-quality regenerative land services with ethical business administration, worker participation, skill development, and eventually democratic worker ownership.

## Values

1. Ecological stewardship
2. Practical service quality
3. Human dignity in work
4. Financial prudence
5. Transparent records and accountable governance
6. Long-term intergenerational thinking
7. Respect for local ecology, land history, and client realities

## Primary offerings

### A. Professional services
- Site assessment and discovery
- Base mapping and land analysis
- Permaculture concept design
- Master planning and phased implementation strategy
- Water management and access planning
- Planting strategy and ecological guild design
- Regenerative farm and homestead redesign
- Restoration and habitat planning
- Project review and implementation advisory support

### B. Educational services
- Workshops and training programs
- Cohort-based learning
- Public speaking and client education
- Digital learning products and reference kits

### C. Digital and intellectual property products
- Templates, calculators, planning tools, and design frameworks
- Proprietary map-editing or design-support assets
- Written guides, visual systems, and licensed educational materials

### D. Field support and operations
- Site walks
- On-site consultation
- Installation coordination or supervision
- Seasonal maintenance guidance and review

## Target customers

1. Homeowners and homesteads
2. Farms seeking regenerative redesign
3. Community organizations and schools
4. Landowners needing ecological restoration planning
5. Clients purchasing digital templates or educational resources

## Revenue model

The Company may earn revenue from:
- fixed-fee design packages,
- hourly or daily consulting,
- retainers,
- workshop fees,
- digital product sales,
- licensing fees,
- implementation support contracts,
- seasonal maintenance or review agreements.

## Operating model

At launch, the business may be founder-led with selective contractor support. Over time it is expected to move toward a team-based structure with documented roles, training pathways, and eventually worker-owner eligibility.

## Core risks

1. Founder overextension
2. Underpricing or scope creep
3. Weak document control
4. Unclear IP ownership of created materials
5. Inconsistent tax and payroll handling
6. Field-work liability and insurance gaps
7. Premature cooperative conversion without administrative readiness

## Required administrative capabilities

To operate responsibly, the Company should maintain:
- signed contracts and scopes,
- invoice and payment records,
- tax registrations and filings,
- insurance records,
- board or member decision records,
- compensation and reimbursement controls,
- IP ownership records,
- website terms and privacy disclosures,
- employee or contractor onboarding documents,
- cooperative conversion readiness metrics.

## Strategic posture

The Company is intentionally designed to begin lean, but not casual. It should behave from day one as a serious operating business whose records and governance can scale into cooperative ownership without rebuilding its administrative foundation from scratch.
